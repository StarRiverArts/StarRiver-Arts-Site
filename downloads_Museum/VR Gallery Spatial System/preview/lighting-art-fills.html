Artistic Fills · 非現實補光

art-first · breaks physics on purpose

① Dual-temp wash

雙色溫橫掃
Two parallel area lights on opposite walls — one cool, one warm — both pointing at the same subject. Physically impossible, visually beautiful: the subject sits inside a temperature gradient.
warm light
3200K · 0.6 · area 200 cm
cool light
5600K · 0.6 · area 200 cm · opposite wall
ambient
0.05 only

② Env-tint floor bounce

自然色地板反彈
Floor is tinted with env-green; an upward-facing area light bounces that colour onto the lower 80 cm of every nearby surface. Implies the gallery is sunk into a forest floor.
floor mat
albedo #9CC069 (env)
up-light
5000K · 0.4 · 80 cm above floor
cap
tint reaches only ≤ 80 cm up

③ Silhouette rim

輪廓鑲邊
Two strip-lights flanking the work, just behind it, edge-on. The work itself stays dark — only its outline glows. Used for works whose silhouette IS the work.
rim L
3200K · 0.9 · 10 cm strip · behind plane
rim R
3200K · 0.9 · 10 cm strip · behind plane
face light
0 · the work is dark on purpose

④ Magenta accent

洋紅點綴
One narrow magenta wash hits a single corner of the room. Never on the subject. Pulls the eye through space and resets the palette before the next gallery.
accent
#C66FA8 · 0.5 · cone 60° · into corner
budget
≤ 1 magenta per gallery
rule
never on body wall behind subject
Why break physics. Real museum lighting prioritises legibility. Virtual museum lighting can also prioritise mood, transition, and pace. These four recipes are licences to disobey the dual-temp / floor-bounce / silhouette / accent boundaries — but each is capped, and never used in combination on a single subject.
