Non-rectangular Spaces · 非方形空間

strategy · 6 archetypes

L · 直角轉折

兩軸線・轉折一次
Treat as two sub-galleries joined at the pivot. Each leg has its own viewing axis. The corner itself is a beat — pause it with a single reserve or open vista.
axis
one per leg
hero
at pivot (peripheral reveal)
light
independent rigs per leg

T · 中央交會

三向軸線・中央樞紐
Three directions meet. The visitor chooses at the hub — design the hub itself as a moment, not a corridor. Hero piece almost always sits in the spur arm.
axis
3 — one per arm
hero
end of spur arm
light
strong hub key + arm fills

Hex · 六角向心

六向放射・中心收束
Six walls, six panels. Centre is the hero locus. Visitor circles continuously — every panel sees every other across the room. Use for a single sculptural work in the middle.
axis
radial · 6 spokes
hero
centre — visible 360°
light
pendant on centre + 6 wall sconces

Oval · 連續環

無角・連續流線
No corners means no decision points. Visitor keeps walking. Works on the perimeter, equally spaced. Use for sequential / serial works whose order is the point.
axis
continuous loop
spacing
even arc-distance (90 cm chord)
light
cove ring at ceiling

Curved partition · 弧形隔間

弧線分區・不封閉
A single curved wall defines two zones without sealing them off. Sight-lines remain open above the curve. Use to imply intimacy without literal enclosure.
axis
2 — one per zone
radius
R 120 / R 240
light

Suite · 多室相連

套間・節奏
Multiple rooms thresholded by short doorways. Reads as scenes. Each room can declare its own lighting climate; the threshold is the cut.
axis
per-room + master thread
rhythm
S → M / hero → S
light
distinct CCT per room
Universal rules for non-rectangular plans.
  1. Decompose into axes first, then walls. If you can't draw a single continuous visiting line through the plan, the plan is wrong.
  2. Every corner is a beat — either a reveal, a pivot, or a hero locus. Never a dead corner.
  3. Curves are not just aesthetic — a partition at R 120 / R 240 changes how the avatar's gaze sweeps the space, vs a hard 90° wall.
  4. If using curved partitions: the wall 「不封閉」 rule — never enclose a space entirely with curves. Always leave one straight wall as the anchor.
  5. Multi-room thresholds should be ≥ 80 cm wide (avatar shoulder + 50 cm comfort) and ≤ 200 cm — wider than 2 m reads as "no threshold" and breaks the scene-cut.
  6. For occlusion, the planner uses every wall edge — non-rect rooms are just walls that happen to form a closed polygon. The shadow polygon math doesn't care.
