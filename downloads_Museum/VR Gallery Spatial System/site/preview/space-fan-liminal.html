Planning · 在 planner 中操作

  1. Pick Circle · 圓形建築 from the room shape picker. This gives you the outer architectural shell of the building as a single circular polygon — every wall placed inside is interior, not perimeter. 先選圓形作為建築外框。
  2. Carve the two fan corridors in Wall mode. Draw solid partition arcs at R 340 cm (or whatever fan-inner radius you want), one across the top, one across the bottom. The arcs leave the inner zone separate from the outer fans. The planner cannot draw true arcs with the wall tool yet — approximate the arc with 6–8 short straight segments at evenly-spaced angles.
  3. Cut the threshold walls: place two short solid walls on each side (east and west), connecting the outer building shell to the end-point of each partition arc. The trapezoidal area between them is the liminal. Leave a 60–80 cm gap between the threshold wall and the partition arc — that's the doorway.
  4. Subdivide the inner zone with voile or solid walls. Voile lets light through but reads as a room boundary; solid encloses fully. A central spine + two flanking rooms gives 3 inner spaces.
  5. Reserves on the outer arc face outward (visitor faces away from centre); reserves on the inner arc face inward (visitor faces centre). Do NOT place both on the same partition wall at the same height — visitors on the other side will see the back of an exhibit.
  6. Lighting climates: Fan A 200–400 lx wash · Liminal 8–15 lx + adapt zones · Fan B 80–150 lx silhouette · Inner rooms hero/spot. The validation panel will report which lights reach each reserve and through which walls (voile passes light, solid blocks).
  7. Verify visibility: select an outer-arc reserve. The validation panel must show "Lit by ≥ 1 light" — if not, place a recessed light near it (track lights work best on curved walls). The Falloff Predictor can sanity-check the rendered colour at the reserve centre.

Why a circular shell · 為什麼用圓形

  1. No dead corners. A circular outer wall has no angles where light or attention pools. The visitor never reaches a corner that demands a decision — they keep walking.
  2. Continuous primary viewing arc. The outer wall is one long, gently-curving surface. Works hang along it; the visitor's gaze sweeps naturally without head-turns.
  3. Symmetry as wayfinding. Two opposing fans + two opposing liminals = perfect rotational symmetry. The visitor's mental map of the building is simple even without signage. 對稱結構自帶導引性。
  4. Inner zone reads as "centre", not "leftover space". A circle's centre is psychologically the destination; treat it as the hero locus of the whole building.
  5. Caveat: the outer wall is harder to render correctly in VR because every ray of light hits the curve at a different angle. Use sparse, evenly-spaced recessed lights (every 90–120 cm of arc), not a single big area light.
